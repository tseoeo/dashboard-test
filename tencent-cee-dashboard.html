Tencent Cloud — CEE Market Sizing Dashboard

Hungary · Bulgaria · Serbia  |  TAM / SAM / SOM Analysis
  1. Combined CEE-3 cloud infrastructure TAM is $1.74 B across ~25,300 enterprise accounts.
  2. Serviceable market (SAM) narrows to $675 M in public IaaS/PaaS — excluding legacy on-prem-only and captive hyperscaler commitments.
  3. Conservative 3-year obtainable market (SOM) is $66 M (~960 accounts), with upside to $106 M in a high scenario.
  4. Mid-to-large enterprises represent 55 % of SAM; tech startups 30 %; government/public sector 15 %.
  5. Brand awareness and local data-center presence are the two highest-impact levers for Tencent's CEE entry.

Methodology

Top-Down Approach

  1. GDP baseline — Start with each country's nominal GDP and apply the IT-spend-to-GDP ratio (~3–4 % for CEE).
  2. Cloud share — Estimate cloud infrastructure as a share of total IT spend (~12–18 % in CEE, growing).
  3. IaaS/PaaS slice — Exclude SaaS and on-prem-only spending to isolate the addressable IaaS + PaaS market = TAM.
  4. Addressability filter (SAM) — Remove captive hyperscaler commitments and legacy-only segments. Apply a 37–40 % filter to derive SAM.
  5. Capture rate (SOM) — Apply a conservative new-entrant 3-year capture rate of 9–10 % of SAM, benchmarked against prior CEE cloud market entries.
  6. Buyer segmentation — Allocate SAM across the three buyer profiles using employment data, startup density, and public-sector IT budget share.

Reconciliation

Cross-checked against Gartner / IDC CEE cloud spending estimates and Eurostat enterprise cloud adoption surveys. Account volumes derived from national business registries filtered to 200+ employee enterprises, Crunchbase startup counts, and public-sector entity counts.

Notes & Glossary

Exclusions

  • SaaS applications (Salesforce, M365, etc.) are excluded from TAM
  • China-specific Tencent services not available in CEE are excluded
  • Consumer cloud storage (personal use) is excluded
  • Managed hosting and colocation-only contracts are excluded
  • Markets outside Hungary, Bulgaria, and Serbia are not covered
